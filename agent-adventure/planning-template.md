# Scenario Plan

## Premise and Player Experience

Describe what the player should feel, notice, and want without exposing future
answers.

## Milestones

For each milestone, list its entry state, meaningful choices, required evidence,
and authoritative exit condition.

## Action and Narration Design

Start with one orientation action. Its completion may reveal up to three short
messages, delayed by at most two seconds. Reserve action buttons for concrete
choices that can change what the player does or learns; fold passive sights,
sounds, bodily sensations, memory state, and immediate affordances into
narration. Mark actions as optional, progression, or utility. Give every
location an exhaustion line and every revealed route an enabled next action.

## World and Choice Graph

List the intended locations and routes. Mark optional investigations, return
paths, and fallback routes that prevent dead ends.

## Resource Budget

List starting resources, passive rates, action costs, finite resupplies, and the
minimum reserve expected at each milestone. Tune continuous rates from measured
route duration, not arbitrary completion deductions.

## First Death Arithmetic

Show at least one complete numeric path to the death boundary. Example:
`100 - 20 - 30 - 50 = 0`. State which actions/effects apply each term and
verify their requirements do not prevent the final boundary.

## Death Reset

List the reset location and exactly which inventory, resources, flags, action
completions, skills, and discoveries persist. Explain how the persistent torn
suit is created before death.

## Part Endpoint

Define the authoritative state required for completion and the meaningful
choices visible at that moment.

## Risks and Fallback Routes

Identify likely player detours, resource exploits, unreachable states, and
what keeps each route playable. List item actions that must remain usable from
Inventory regardless of location.
